# Day 2 Report – Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) & Business Insights

## Project: Mutual Fund Analytics Capstone

### Objective

The objective of Day 2 was to perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the mutual fund datasets loaded during Day 1. The analysis focused on understanding fund performance, investor behavior, SIP growth trends, portfolio composition, and industry-level investment patterns.

---

# Datasets Used

The following datasets were analyzed:

1. Scheme Performance
2. Monthly SIP Inflows
3. Investor Transactions
4. Portfolio Holdings
5. Fund Master
6. Benchmark Indices

---

# Analysis Performed

## 1. Top Mutual Funds by Assets Under Management (AUM)

### Objective

Identify the largest mutual fund schemes based on Assets Under Management (AUM).

### Methodology

The Scheme Performance dataset was sorted in descending order using the `aum_crore` field.

### Findings

* Large-cap and diversified equity funds dominate total industry assets.
* The top 10 schemes collectively represent a significant share of total AUM.
* Investors continue to favor established schemes with long performance histories.

### Business Insight

Higher AUM often reflects stronger investor trust, brand recognition, and fund manager credibility. Large-cap funds remain the preferred investment vehicle for retail investors.

---

## 2. SIP Growth Trend Analysis

### Objective

Analyze the growth of Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) contributions over time.

### Dataset

Monthly SIP Inflows

### Findings

* Monthly SIP inflows increased from approximately ₹11,500 Crore to over ₹31,000 Crore.
* SIP participation showed a steady upward trend throughout the observed period.
* Growth accelerated significantly after 2023.

### Business Insight

The consistent increase in SIP inflows demonstrates growing retail investor confidence and a shift toward disciplined long-term investing.

### Key Observation

The mutual fund industry is experiencing sustained retail participation growth, making SIPs one of the strongest drivers of industry expansion.

---

## 3. Investor Demographics Analysis

### Objective

Understand investor behavior and participation patterns.

### Variables Analyzed

* Age Group
* Gender
* City Tier
* Payment Mode

---

### Age Distribution

Analysis of transaction activity by age group revealed that investment participation is concentrated within selected working-age demographics.

#### Insight

The majority of investment activity originates from economically active individuals with long-term wealth creation objectives.

---

### Gender Distribution

Transaction activity was analyzed across gender categories.

#### Insight

The dataset indicates varying participation rates between male and female investors, highlighting opportunities for targeted investor outreach programs.

---

### City Tier Analysis

Investors were segmented into:

* Tier 1 Cities
* Tier 2 Cities
* Tier 3 Cities

#### Findings

* Tier 1 cities account for the largest share of investment activity.
* Tier 2 cities also contribute significantly.
* Mutual fund adoption is expanding beyond major metropolitan regions.

#### Business Insight

Growing participation from Tier 2 cities indicates expanding market penetration and increasing financial awareness across India.

---

### Payment Mode Analysis

Payment methods analyzed:

* UPI
* Net Banking
* Mandate
* Cheque
* Other digital channels

#### Findings

Digital payment methods dominate transaction activity.

#### Business Insight

Investors increasingly prefer frictionless digital investment experiences, reinforcing the importance of fintech-enabled distribution channels.

# 4. State-wise Investment Analysis

### Objective

Identify the states contributing the highest investment volumes.

### Methodology

Total investment amounts were aggregated by state using investor transaction data.

### Top States by Investment Amount

1. Tamil Nadu
2. Punjab
3. Madhya Pradesh
4. Rajasthan
5. Gujarat
6. West Bengal
7. Telangana
8. Delhi
9. Uttar Pradesh
10. Haryana

### Findings

* Investment activity is concentrated within a limited number of states.
* Southern, Northern, and Western regions contribute strongly to industry inflows.

### Business Insight

These states represent high-potential markets for mutual fund companies seeking customer acquisition and deeper market penetration.

### Strategic Recommendation

Fund houses should prioritize investor education campaigns and distribution expansion in the highest-contributing states while exploring growth opportunities in underpenetrated regions.

---

# 5. Portfolio Sector Exposure Analysis

### Objective

Understand sector allocation patterns across mutual fund portfolios.

### Methodology

Portfolio holdings were aggregated by sector and analyzed using total portfolio weights.

### Top Sectors by Portfolio Weight

1. Banking
2. Information Technology (IT)
3. Pharma
4. Automobile
5. Utilities

### Additional Significant Sectors

* FMCG
* Infrastructure
* Diversified
* Telecom
* Consumer Goods

### Findings

* Banking represents the largest sector allocation.
* IT remains the second-largest portfolio exposure.
* Pharma maintains a significant share of portfolio allocations.

### Business Insight

Fund managers maintain strong exposure to sectors considered critical to India's economic growth and market performance.

### Risk Observation

Sector concentration risk exists because Banking and IT collectively represent a substantial portion of portfolio allocations. Market downturns affecting these sectors could significantly impact portfolio performance.

---

# Key Insights

## Investor Behavior

* Retail participation continues to increase.
* SIP adoption is growing rapidly.
* Digital investment channels dominate transaction activity.

## Geographic Trends

* Investment activity is concentrated in a small number of high-contributing states.
* Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities remain the primary growth drivers.

## Fund Industry Trends

* Large-cap funds continue to dominate Assets Under Management.
* Investors show strong preference for established mutual fund schemes.

## Portfolio Strategy

* Banking and IT sectors receive the highest portfolio allocations.
* Portfolio diversification exists but concentration risk should be monitored.

---

# Business Recommendations

### 1. Expand SIP Adoption

Mutual fund companies should continue promoting SIP-based investing through investor education and digital engagement campaigns.

### 2. Strengthen Digital Channels

Given the dominance of digital payment methods, investment platforms should focus on improving user experience and digital onboarding.

### 3. Geographic Expansion

Fund houses should target emerging markets while maintaining strong engagement in leading states such as Tamil Nadu, Punjab, Gujarat, and Rajasthan.

### 4. Monitor Sector Concentration

Portfolio managers should regularly assess concentration risk in Banking and IT sectors to maintain diversification.

### 5. Increase Investor Awareness

Financial literacy programs can help expand participation among underserved demographics and regions.

---

# Conclusion

The Day 2 Exploratory Data Analysis reveals a healthy and expanding mutual fund ecosystem characterized by increasing SIP adoption, strong retail participation, growing digital engagement, and significant geographic concentration. Banking and IT continue to dominate portfolio allocations, while large-cap schemes maintain leadership in Assets Under Management. Overall, the findings indicate strong long-term growth potential for the Indian mutual fund industry and provide actionable insights for fund houses, investors, and financial service providers.
